Drinking Atlas

Top 10 Aperitifs & Where They’re From

A quick cheat sheet for bar teams comparing the classics. Each bottle brings a different viewpoint on bitterness, sweetness, and botanicals across Italy, France, and beyond.

# Aperitif Origin Profile
01 Aperol Padua, Italy Sunset-orange, bitter orange + rhubarb, low ABV spritz staple.
02 Campari Milan, Italy Bold bitter backbone for Negronis; gentian, cherry, quinine.
03 Lillet Blanc Podensac, France White Bordeaux fortified with citrus liqueurs; floral, honeyed finish.
04 Suze Pontarlier, France Electric yellow gentian bitter; earthy, peppery bite.
05 Cynar Veneto, Italy Artichoke-based amaro with cocoa + cola notes; fantastic with soda.
06 Select Aperitivo Venice, Italy Classic Venetian spritz bitter—juniper, rhubarb, vanilla.
07 Ramazzotti Rosato Milan, Italy Bright hibiscus + orange blossom; built for sparkling wine.
08 Byrrh Grand Quinquina Thuir, France Red wine quinquina with dark fruit, spice, and cinchona bark.
09 Pimm’s No. 1 London, England Gin-based fruit cup; cucumber, citrus, soft spice—summer cups essential.
10 Gancia Americano Rosso Canelli, Italy Herbal red bitter with alpine botanicals; bridges vermouth + amaro.
